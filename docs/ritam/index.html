What RITAM Is

Nine primitives. Each justified by a failure mode.

RITAM is foundational substrate research. It investigates the structural conditions a governed cognition system requires — not as design aspirations, but as experimentally justified necessities. Remove any one primitive and a specific, observable failure appears.

The founding principle: governance must precede persistence. An AI system that admits beliefs without governance and attempts to clean them up afterward is not a governed system — it is an ungoverned system with remediation. RITAM sits beneath the model and beneath the application, governing cognitive state at the moment of admission.

The Investigation

What RITAM is working to answer

Is multi-primitive superadditivity — the finding that simultaneous governance degradations compound worse than their sum — architecture-independent, or does it require specific substrate conditions?
Where does each of the nine primitives attach in a running system? What observable signatures confirm it is functioning?
What is the minimum viable executable substrate — the threshold at which NIYOM can begin implementation?
Do RITAM's core empirical findings represent genuinely new theory, or a governance-framed restatement of established results in adjacent fields?
Research Foundation

Built on the published papers

RITAM is grounded in the specific findings of eight published papers. The architecture is not speculative — it is derived from documented observations. Every design decision in RITAM should be traceable to a finding in the research.

RITAM's own experimental programme has also produced findings documented independently of the published papers — pre-registered experiments under adversarial review, and a prior-art audit confirming that the core findings represent genuinely new contributions to the literature.

Primary foundations:

Paper 01 · Reliability Stack (A1–A10) Paper 02 · Operational Protocols Paper 03 · Failure Taxonomy Paper 06 · Six-Layer Architecture Paper 07 · Distributed Cognition Paper 08 · Verification Systems
Relationship to NIYOM

Architecture and execution

RITAM and NIYOM are two distinct but dependent projects. RITAM is architecture: it specifies what a reliable collaboration system looks like structurally. NIYOM is execution: it implements those specifications as practical tools and protocols.

RITAM has to exist first. The architecture has to be right before implementation is useful. NIYOM follows RITAM's output, not the other way around.

Current Status

v1.1.1 is live. What that means.

The runtime substrate is published open source under Apache 2.0. 146 tests pass across adversarial, integration, and buildability scenarios on Python 3.9, 3.10, and 3.11. Five independent AI systems reproduced the runtime from the specification alone — 60/60 tests passing — confirming the specification is precise enough to build from without guidance from the original author.

Three consumer types are demonstrated: governed research assistant, governed decision log, governed agent memory. The substrate is domain-agnostic — the same nine primitives govern cognitive state regardless of what the application does with it.

The canonical specification (nine chapters) continues in active development. The runtime demonstrates that the architecture is correct and buildable. The specification provides the theoretical account of why it is the right architecture — and what it must remain to stay right.

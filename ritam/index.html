RITAM

A practical architecture for reliable long-horizon human-AI collaboration — translating eight papers of foundational research into structural form.

The gap this addresses

The published research established that reliable AI collaboration requires structural conditions — not better models. RITAM is the attempt to specify what that structure actually looks like in practice.

What RITAM Is

From research to architecture

Eight papers documented the problem. RITAM is the next step: taking those findings and building an architecture that instantiates them.

The papers established several things clearly. That AI reliability is structural, not intrinsic to the model. That failure modes are predictable and recurring. That the absence of a dedicated verification layer is the central outstanding problem. That language, memory, and governance structure all function as reliability mechanisms.

RITAM asks: what does an architecture that takes all of this seriously actually look like? What are its components? How do they interact? What does it require from the human side, and what does it require from the system side?

The Investigation

What RITAM is working to answer

How do the ten reliability controls from Paper 01 interact as a system — and what does their interaction require architecturally?
What does the six-layer governance architecture from Paper 06 look like when implemented, layer by layer?
Where does the verification layer attach in the architecture — and what does it need in order to function independently of the generation process it is verifying?
How does a persistent memory architecture need to be structured to prevent Context Drift and Reconstruction Failure over long collaboration timelines?
What are the minimum structural conditions for a human-AI collaboration system to be considered reliably governed?
Research Foundation

Built on the published papers

RITAM is grounded in the specific findings of eight published papers. The architecture is not speculative — it is derived from documented observations. Every design decision in RITAM should be traceable to a finding in the research.

Primary foundations:

Paper 01 · Reliability Stack (A1–A10) Paper 02 · Operational Protocols Paper 03 · Failure Taxonomy Paper 06 · Six-Layer Architecture Paper 07 · Distributed Cognition Paper 08 · Verification Systems
Relationship to NIYOM

Architecture and execution

RITAM and NIYOM are two distinct but dependent projects. RITAM is architecture: it specifies what a reliable collaboration system looks like structurally. NIYOM is execution: it implements those specifications as practical tools and protocols.

RITAM has to exist first. The architecture has to be right before implementation is useful. NIYOM follows RITAM's output, not the other way around.

Current Status

Active development · 2026

RITAM is in active development. The research foundation is complete — eight papers provide the empirical and theoretical basis. The architecture work itself is ongoing: specifying components, mapping interactions, establishing what the minimum viable reliability architecture looks like before NIYOM can implement it.

RITAM is not a product. It is architecture research. Its output is a specification, not a piece of software.

RITAM

Foundational substrate research for governed AI cognition. RITAM investigates the nine architectural primitives an executable governed cognition system requires — and holds itself to a single constraint: they must work as observable runtime behaviour, not just specification.

The constraint RITAM holds itself to

A specification that doesn't run doesn't count. The research programme has run 76 sessions, pre-registered experiments, and an adversarial review tier staffed by AI personas briefed to attack the core findings. The runtime directory is deliberately empty — that gap is the accountability mechanism.

What RITAM Is

Nine primitives. One constraint: they must run.

RITAM is foundational substrate research — not a practical tool, not a product, not a translation project. It investigates the structural conditions an executable governed cognition system requires.

The research has converged on nine architectural primitives: State, Memory, Ontology, Governance, Epistemic, Coordination, Temporal, Observation, Repair. These are not design aspirations. They are the components RITAM's experiments have identified as necessary before a substrate can maintain coherence, detect degradation, and repair itself under real operating conditions.

Every primitive must be observable at runtime, testable under adversarial conditions, and provably necessary before it earns a place in the specification.

The Investigation

What RITAM is working to answer

Is multi-primitive superadditivity — the finding that simultaneous governance degradations compound worse than their sum — architecture-independent, or does it require specific substrate conditions?
Where does each of the nine primitives attach in a running system? What observable signatures confirm it is functioning?
What is the minimum viable executable substrate — the threshold at which NIYOM can begin implementation?
Do RITAM's core empirical findings represent genuinely new theory, or a governance-framed restatement of established results in adjacent fields?
Research Foundation

Built on the published papers

RITAM is grounded in the specific findings of eight published papers. The architecture is not speculative — it is derived from documented observations. Every design decision in RITAM should be traceable to a finding in the research.

RITAM's own experimental programme has also produced findings documented independently of the published papers — 74 pre-registered experiments, an adversarial review tier, and an honest prior-art audit completed at Session 74.

Primary foundations:

Paper 01 · Reliability Stack (A1–A10) Paper 02 · Operational Protocols Paper 03 · Failure Taxonomy Paper 06 · Six-Layer Architecture Paper 07 · Distributed Cognition Paper 08 · Verification Systems
Relationship to NIYOM

Architecture and execution

RITAM and NIYOM are two distinct but dependent projects. RITAM is architecture: it specifies what a reliable collaboration system looks like structurally. NIYOM is execution: it implements those specifications as practical tools and protocols.

RITAM has to exist first. The architecture has to be right before implementation is useful. NIYOM follows RITAM's output, not the other way around.

Current Status

Session 76 · Stage 9 · Re-anchoring, 2026

Nine chapters of the canonical specification are in late draft. The experimental programme has run 74 pre-registered experiments with pre-specified hypotheses and an adversarial review tier. The core empirical finding — superadditivity of governance failures — has been tested, challenged by adversarial review, and subjected to a private prior-art audit.

The runtime doesn't exist yet. This is not a gap to paper over — it is the accountability mechanism the project holds itself to. The NORTH_STAR document is explicit: a polished specification that doesn't run doesn't count as success.

RITAM is not a product. It is foundational infrastructure research.
